Dive Sites

Discover Camiguin's Underwater World

Six unique dive sites surround Camiguin Island — from marine sanctuary reefs teeming with turtles to adrenaline-pumping drift dives with pelagics.

Most Popular

Mantigue Island

📍 15 min by boat 🌊 Depth: 5–25m 🏅 All levels 👁️ Visibility: 15–30m

Mantigue Island Marine Sanctuary is Camiguin's crown jewel. This tiny island is surrounded by a vibrant reef that is home to an extraordinary population of green sea turtles. On a single dive, it's common to encounter 5–10 turtles grazing on the reef.

The gentle slope of the reef makes it perfect for divers of all levels, while the rich biodiversity — from schooling jackfish and barracuda to colorful nudibranchs and anemone gardens — keeps experienced divers coming back.

What You'll See

🐢 Green Sea Turtles 🐟 Jackfish Schools 🐠 Nudibranchs 🐡 Barracuda 🪸 Anemone Garden 🦑 Cuttlefish
Advanced

Burias Shoal

📍 30 min by boat 🌊 Depth: 15–40m+ 🏅 Advanced divers 💨 Strong current

Burias Shoal is Camiguin's most exhilarating dive. Located in the open ocean between Camiguin and Bohol, this submerged reef attracts big pelagic action. Strong currents sweep across the shoal, bringing nutrient-rich water that draws large schools of fish and apex predators.

This is where you come for the big encounters — thresher sharks, occasional manta rays, schools of trevally, and barracuda walls. Recommended for Advanced Open Water certified divers comfortable with current dives.

What You'll See

🦈 Thresher Sharks 🦈 Manta Rays (seasonal) 🐟 Trevally Schools 🐡 Barracuda Walls 🐢 Turtles 💨 Drift Dive
Unique

Jigdup Shoal

📍 20 min by boat 🌊 Depth: 13–30m 🏅 Intermediate+ 🕳️ Swim-through cave

Jigdup Shoal features Camiguin's most unique underwater topography — a swim-through cave that takes you from 18 meters up to 13 meters through a dramatic rock formation. Light streams in from the exit as you glide through, creating unforgettable photo opportunities.

Outside the cave, the reef is a hotspot for critters: frogfish hide among the sponges, large schools of snapper patrol the reef edge, and whip corals host tiny gobies and shrimp.

What You'll See

🕳️ Swim-through Cave 🐸 Frogfish 🐟 Snapper Schools 🦐 Cleaner Shrimp 🪸 Whip Corals
Scenic

White Island

📍 10 min by boat 🌊 Depth: 5–20m 🏅 All levels 👁️ Visibility: 15–25m

White Island is Camiguin's iconic sandbar, and beneath the turquoise waters lies a beautiful coral garden. The dive site features impressive black coral trees, vast fields of staghorn coral, and the occasional banded sea snake gliding through the reef.

The gentle conditions and varied topography make this an excellent site for underwater photography and relaxed diving. The combination of the surreal sandbar above and the rich reef below makes for a truly unique Camiguin experience.

What You'll See

🪸 Black Coral Trees 🪸 Staghorn Coral Fields 🐍 Sea Snakes 🐠 Reef Fish 📷 Great for Photos
Macro Paradise

Cabuan / Giant Clam

📍 Shore dive 🌊 Depth: 3–18m 🏅 All levels 📷 Macro diving

This shore dive is Camiguin's macro paradise and home to giant Tridacna clams reaching up to one meter across. The clams are the star attraction, with their iridescent mantles creating psychedelic patterns that mesmerize underwater photographers.

Beyond the clams, the rubble and sandy reef is rich with critters — nudibranchs, seahorses, pipefish, and a variety of shrimp. A perfect site for a slow-paced, discovery dive where every square meter reveals something new.

What You'll See

🐚 Giant Clams (1m+) 📷 Macro Critters 🐌 Nudibranchs 🐎 Seahorses 🦐 Shrimp Species
Historic

Sunken Cemetery

📍 10 min by boat 🌊 Depth: 5–20m 🏅 All levels 🌋 Volcanic origin

One of the most unique dive sites in the Philippines. In 1871, the eruption of Mount Vulcan caused the old town of Catarman to sink into the sea, taking its cemetery with it. Today, a large cross marks the spot above the water, while below, coral-encrusted tombstones and ruins create an eerie, hauntingly beautiful dive.

The site attracts schools of batfish that circle the cross, green sea turtles resting on the reef, and a variety of reef fish that have made the old cemetery their home. A dive here is both a historical and marine experience.

What You'll See

🌋 1871 Volcanic Ruins 🦇 Batfish Schools 🐢 Sea Turtles ✝️ Coral-encrusted Tombs 🐠 Reef Fish
