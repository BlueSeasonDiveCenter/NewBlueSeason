Camiguin Dive Sites — The 6 Best Diving Spots in Camiguin Island

Six unique dive sites surround Camiguin Island, Philippines — from marine sanctuary reefs teeming with sea turtles to adrenaline-pumping drift dives with pelagics. Dive all of them with Blue Season, your PADI dive center in Camiguin.

Most Popular

Mantigue Island

📍 30 min by boat (~10 km) 🌊 Depth: 5–25m 🏅 All levels 👁️ Visibility: 15–30m

The waters around Mantigue Island are part of a protected marine sanctuary, featuring healthy coral gardens with both hard and soft corals, sandy slopes and reef edges suitable for relaxed dives. Excellent visibility often ranges from 15 to 30 meters, and mild currents create a safe and visually rewarding environment for divers of all levels.

Divers can expect to encounter a wide variety of marine species, including reef fish such as clownfish, angelfish, and butterflyfish. More than 20 green sea turtles call this sanctuary home, with occasional sightings of impressively large individuals. Moray eels, reef squid, and nudibranchs are also common, as are schools of pelagic fish along the reef edges. The protected status of the area ensures a thriving and well-preserved underwater ecosystem.

Unique

Jigdup Shoal

📍 Northeastern Camiguin 🌊 Depth: 5–30m+ 🏅 All levels 🪸 Slope & Wall

Located on the northeastern side of Camiguin, Jigdup covers two dive sites — Jigdup Slope and Jigdup Wall — making it one of the island's most vibrant and biologically rich destinations. Jigdup stands out for its excellent coral health, schooling fish, and classic tropical reef scenery that suits divers of all levels.

Jigdup Slope usually starts as a gentle coral slope around 5–15 meters, featuring extensive hard coral formations including table corals and brain corals, then gradually drops into a deeper wall. Colorful soft corals and sea fans line the deeper sections, offering both shallow reef exploration and rewarding deeper dives in a single site.

Jigdup Wall is a stunning dive site where the reef starts at around 5 meters and drops steeply to about 40 meters. Near the end of the dive, there is a small open cave along the reef, extending vertically from about 18 to 13 meters, adding an interesting feature to explore.

Scenic

White Island

📍 40 min by boat 🌊 Depth: 6–25m 🏅 All levels 👁️ Visibility: 15–25m

White Island is not only a striking white sandbar but also a rewarding dive site known for its reef slopes and rich marine biodiversity. Reached in about 40 minutes by boat from our Camiguin dive center, beneath its calm, crystal-clear surface lies an enchanting underwater landscape, including notable black coral gardens that attract a wide variety of marine species.

White Island offers a gently sloping reef that begins in shallow water around 6 meters and gradually descends to about 25 meters, making it suitable for divers of all levels. The site is well known for its impressive black coral "forest," interspersed with healthy hard and soft coral formations that create a rich and textured underwater landscape. Visibility is typically good, often ranging from 15 to 30 meters, and mild to moderate currents can turn the dive into an enjoyable drift along the reef.

As you move along the slope, you'll encounter coral gardens, scattered bommies, and open sandy patches, with the reef structure providing plenty of overhangs, crevices, and hiding spots for marine life.

Advanced

Burias Shoal

📍 2 km north of Mantigue 🌊 Depth: 15–40m+ 🏅 Advanced divers 💨 Moderate to strong current

Burias Shoal is an offshore reef that rises from deeper water like a submerged seamount, creating a dynamic and exposed dive site. The top features coral bommies and reef patches with sandy channels in between, while the edges slope down into the blue. The structure is covered in healthy hard and soft corals, along with a distinctive black coral "forest," giving the site rich texture and depth.

Currents are often present — ranging from moderate to strong — bringing nutrient-rich water across the reef and making this site especially known for big fish action and frequent pelagic encounters. Due to its conditions and exposure, it is recommended for Advanced Open Water divers only.

What You'll See

🐟 Schools of Jack Fish & Big Eye 🐡 Barracuda & Tuna 🦈 Occasional Thresher Sharks 🦈 Reef Sharks 🪸 Black Coral Forest 🌸 Soft Corals & Sea Fans

Best Suited For

🎓 Advanced Divers 💨 Drift Diving 🔭 Pelagic Encounters

⚠️ Note: Currents can be strong and unpredictable — good buoyancy and dive control required. Reef hooks may be helpful in certain conditions.

Historic

Sunken Cemetery

📍 Western coast of Camiguin 🌊 Depth: 5–20m 🏅 All levels 🌋 1871 Volcanic origin

Located off the western coast of Camiguin, the Sunken Cemetery is one of the Philippines' most atmospheric and historically unique dive sites. In 1871, an eruption of Mount Vulcan caused the coastline to subside, submerging the old town of Catarman — and its cemetery — beneath the sea. A large cross rising above the water marks the site today.

Underwater, divers can explore scattered gravestones, stone walls, and remnants of the old structure, now partially covered by corals and marine growth. Over time, these submerged ruins have developed into an artificial reef, with soft corals, sponges, and patches of hard corals creating small but thriving ecosystems. The site is generally calm, with gentle conditions and a slightly silty seabed, giving it a quiet, surreal, and contemplative diving experience.

The combination of history and marine life makes this site especially unique, where encrusted ruins blend seamlessly with reef life, offering scenic photography opportunities.

Macro Paradise

Cabuan / Giant Clam

📍 Southeast coast of Camiguin 🌊 Depth: 3–18m 🏅 All levels 📷 Macro diving

Located off the southeast coast of Camiguin, Cabuan is best known as a giant clam sanctuary — one of the most ecologically distinctive dive sites on the island. Of the seven types of giant clams in the world, six can be found in Camiguin, making it one of the most unique ecological dive sites in the region.

Cabuan is a protected reef site featuring a mix of soft coral, reef rubble, and sandy patches, creating a varied habitat that supports both macro life and larger reef activity. It is also an active conservation zone for giant clams, helping support their recovery in the region. The shallow, accessible reef makes it a relaxed and rewarding dive for all levels.

The star attraction is Cabuan's population of giant Tridacna clams — the largest living bivalves in the world, growing to over a meter in length. Their iridescent mantles display striking patterns of blue, green, and brown under sunlight. Beyond the clams, the reef rewards patient divers with a rich variety of macro life, making it both unique and photogenic for macro enthusiasts.
